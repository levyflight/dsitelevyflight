Abhagi’s Heaven, Sarat Chandra Chattopadhyay

Sat Apr 04, 2020 Categories: Bengali-Short-Story / Inequality . Tags: Sarat Chandra Chattopadhyay .

I THAKURDAS Mukherjee’s ageing wife died of fever after seven days’ illness. Old Mukhopadhyaya had amassed a vast fortune by trading in paddy. He had four sons and three daughters,who also had their own children. Then there were the sons-in-law, neighbours, servants and maids — it all felt like acarnival. The whole village came crowding in to watch the funeral procession, now taking place with great pomp and ceremony. The weeping women smeared their mother’s feet with a deep coat of alta[red dye] and her head with sindur[vermilion], while the daughters-in-law anointed the forehead of their mother-in-law with sandalwood paste and, wiping the last trace of dust off her feet with the loose ends of their saris,draped her body in rich, costly clothes.
